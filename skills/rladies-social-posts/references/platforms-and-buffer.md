# Social media: platforms, Canva templates, Buffer & amplification reference

Deep reference for `rladies-social-posts`. Sources: `global-team/social-media-management`,
`community/twitter`, `community/iwd`, `branding/canva-social-media`.

## Canva workspace & brand templates

The [RLadies+ Canva workspace](https://www.canva.com/folder/FAHCPq0DH3w) holds the
brand templates and assets, published as **Brand Templates** and organised by content
type. Use Brand ▸ Brand templates ▸ _Use this template_ (makes your own copy) → pick
the page sized for your platform → edit text/images → export PNG (GIF for animation).

| Template                    | Use for                                    |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Standard - Logo + Image     | General posts with the R+ logo and a photo |
| Text & Logo                 | Text-focused posts with the logo           |
| Text & Image inside Logo    | A photo filling the R+ logo shape          |
| Photo background & Quote    | Full photo with a quote/message overlay    |
| Announcement - Image + Text | Event or news announcements                |
| Event Announcements         | Meetup and event promotions                |
| RoCur                       | Rotating curator intro/feature posts       |

Workspace folders: `Social Media/Posts`, `Social Media/Covers`, `RoCur`, `Rebranding`,
`Assets` (editable R+ logo and brand elements), `Archive`.

**Brand Kit** holds the official colours, fonts, and logos — use those, never custom
values. **Do not** change the fonts, colours, or layout of template elements; only edit
placeholder text, images, and logos. Export: Share → Download → PNG/JPG (GIF for
animated).

Available canvas formats: posts in **1:1, 4:5, 9:16, 16:9**; banners/covers in
**3:1, 4:1, 16:9**.

## Buffer details

Connected channels: Bluesky, Mastodon, LinkedIn, Instagram (not YouTube). Contact the
communications team lead for access; default permission is **Approval Required** (a
lead approves before publishing).

Steps: log in to <https://publish.buffer.com/> → Create Post → select channels → write
text (keep brand voice) → upload the correctly-sized image per platform → set date/time
→ **Schedule Post**, or **Add to Queue** for evergreen content at optimal weekly times.
Tips: tailor text per platform (hashtags, mentions); always preview each platform first.

## Global @rladies account — full nuance

**Retweets:**

- Chapter posts about past events — not all of them; the best are those with pics
  and/or links to resources, ideally with more RLadies+ in frame.
- Fun chapter posts (e.g. cookies baked for an event).
- The Monday WeAreRLadies new-curator announcement and that account's first intro post,
  plus relevant posts that week (not all).
- Posts informatively describing a future **online** event (chapter launches are more
  locally relevant — skip).
- Posts about RLadies+ output (blogs, packages, etc.), ideally with a link; old
  resources are fine.
- Other languages welcome once translated — and important to share.
- No ads — a book you can't read online for free is a no-go.

To tell if someone is an R-Lady: ask, check their profile (maybe `#rladies` is in their
bio), or look them up in the directory.

**Likes** — lower stakes than retweets but still a form of approval; the account can
like more event posts than it retweets, including about future events.

**Don't retweet:**

- Future in-person chapter events / local feedback requests (local audience only).
- Job postings.
- Photos that just look like a generic R User Group.
- Ads (incl. non-free books).
- The least inclusive "women"-hashtag posts (chapter posts that include them are ok —
  just avoid the least inclusive ones).

**Scope** — unlike R-Forwards, the account is very R-focused and rarely retweets
general feminism / feminism-in-tech; the narrow scope keeps decisions easy.

**Content review** — anything not positive (e.g. taking a stance on a controversy) is
always reviewed by global leadership/team.

**DMs / mentions** — not a Q&A account; don't answer or retweet R questions. Redirect:

```
This account doesn't do Q&A, you should join our community Slack to ask such questions 🙂

https://rladies.org/form/community-slack/
```

Ignore trolls (ask when in doubt). Mute threads where the tag was attention-seeking or
a hashtag-vs-tag mix-up. Job mention: ignore if too busy, otherwise paste to the Slack
jobs channel with a note that you don't endorse the ad.

## IWD campaign history (reference)

Infrastructure is just the [`@rladies_iwd`](https://twitter.com/rladies_iwd) account,
linked to `iwd@rladies.org`.

- **2018** — featured every R-Lady from the directory; take-home: sign up in the
  directory.
- **2019** — featured every chapter; take-home: how to create a chapter.
- **2020** — featured RLadies+ lessons (with a Shiny app); take-home: share and reuse
  meetup materials.

built for/by RLadies+.
