# RLadies+ Social Media

Write everything in the [RLadies+ voice](../rladies-voice/SKILL.md): warm and direct,
"we"/"our", spotlight people and chapters, one exclamation mark max, 💜 as the
signature emoji, and **"gender minorities in R"** (the inclusive-language rule,
in `rladies-brand`) — never "females" or "women only". Run drafts through
`rladies-voice`'s tell list to strip stock openers, generic CTAs, and other
AI-sounding patterns before scheduling. Posts about **controversies,
partnerships, or policy changes must be reviewed by the leadership team before
publishing.**

The **WeAreRLadies** rotating curation runs separately on Bluesky; IWD campaigns use a
separate `@rladies_iwd` account.

## Cadence, timing, accessibility

- **2–3 posts per week** on each platform.
- Best reach **weekdays 14:00–16:00 UTC**.
- **Always add alt text** describing the image, including any text shown in it.
- Hashtags: `#RLadies`, `#RStats`, plus event-specific tags (`#PositConf`, `#useR`,
  `#LatinR`).

## Scheduling with Buffer

The RLadies+ Buffer account is connected to **Bluesky, Mastodon, LinkedIn, and
Instagram** (YouTube is manual). New team members are added with **Approval Required**,
so a lead approves posts before they go live. In Buffer: Create Post → pick channels →
write text (per-platform tailoring for hashtags/mentions) → upload the correctly-sized
image → set date/time → **Schedule Post** (or **Add to Queue** for evergreen content
at optimal times). Always preview each platform before scheduling.

## Curating the global @rladies account

The account is **very R-focused** — it rarely shares general feminism/feminism-in-tech
content; the narrow scope is deliberate.

**Amplify (retweet/share):**

- Chapter posts about **past** events — favour ones with photos and/or links to
  resources; more RLadies+ in frame is better.
- Fun chapter posts (e.g. cookies baked for an event).
- The Monday post announcing the new WeAreRLadies curator and their intro post (plus
  relevant posts from that account that week — not all of them).
- Posts that informatively describe a **future online** event (not chapter launches,
  which are locally relevant).
- Posts about things RLadies+ do — blog posts, packages, etc. — ideally with a link;
  old resources are fine.
- Other languages are welcome (translate first) and important to share.

**Don't amplify:**

- Future **in-person** chapter events, or calls for local feedback (local audience only).
- Job postings.
- Photos where it just looks like a generic R User Group.
- Anything that reads as an **ad** — a book you can't read online for free is a no-go.
- The least inclusive "women"-hashtag posts (chapter posts that happen to use them are
  ok).

**Mentions & DMs:** we're not a Q&A account — don't answer or amplify R questions;
redirect to the community Slack. Ignore trolls; mute attention-seeking threads. For job
mentions, ignore if busy or drop into the Slack jobs channel with a no-endorsement note.

The exact retweet/like nuances and the redirect template are in
[references/platforms-and-buffer.md](references/platforms-and-buffer.md).

## International Women's Day campaigns

RLadies+ has run IWD campaigns on the dedicated `@rladies_iwd` account (linked to
`iwd@rladies.org`). To launch one: get the global team's OK in Slack and request
account access, then:

- Set a clear goal aligned with the mission (note: the mission covers all minority
  genders; IWD is the occasion, not the framing).
- Use a separate account so followers aren't flooded.
- Build a **team** of volunteers and a clear task list.
- Automate and **test** the posting flow (manual is sometimes faster — no prize for
  doing it the hard way).
- Prepare comms **before** (what we'll cover and why) and **after** (the take-home and
  where to re-read the content).
- **Get permission** before using anyone's content or photos.

Past campaign themes and ideas are in
[references/platforms-and-buffer.md](references/platforms-and-buffer.md).
