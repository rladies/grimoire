# Moderating the RLadies+ Community

The Community Slack is a **safe, global space for people who identify as a woman or
gender minority** and are interested in R — to discuss #rstats news, packages, jobs,
events, and ideas. (Cis men are asked **not** to sign up; the gender scope is what makes
it a safe space.) People join via <https://rladies.org/form/community-slack/>. It's
distinct from the **Organizers' Slack** (chapter organizers only).

## Moderation guidelines

- **Welcome** newcomers and encourage them to **introduce themselves**.
- **Keep channels focused** — nudge off-topic chat to the right channel (there's always
  `#random`); open a new channel when a recurring off-topic theme grows, and
  **combine/close** near-empty channels so the list doesn't sprawl.
- **Encourage threads** to keep conversations readable.
- **Discourage `@channel`** — `@here` only pings active people, so prefer it.
- Point people to `#how_to_slack` for Slack help, and ensure the Code of Conduct is
  respected throughout.

## Inclusive language

Encourage **gender-neutral language**: avoid addressing people as **"ladies"** or
**"guys"** — use **"folks", "y'all", "everyone", "everybody", "friends"** or **"pals"**.
This is the Slack-specific phrasing; the broader inclusive-language rule ("gender
minorities in R") is in `rladies-brand`, and the wider RLadies+ tone for any reply
you draft is in `rladies-voice`.

## What's allowed (and not)

**Allowed:** asking/answering questions, sharing achievements, jobs, events/conferences,
#rstats news, resources, and community ideas. **Not allowed:** promoting commercial
products (a member discount must be cleared with leadership first) and inappropriate
content (disturbing media, false information, offensive/mocking content). **Unsure about a
post?** Ask the **`@Community Management Team`** in Slack or email **`slack@rladies.org`**.

## Code of Conduct & reporting

The [Code of Conduct](https://rladies.org/coc/) applies to **all RLadies+ spaces**
(meetups, social, Slack, mailing lists — online and offline). On Slack specifically: don't
share content posted in Slack outside it without the person's consent, and avoid
sexual/offensive usernames or profile photos.

**To report harassment or a concern**: email **`reporting@rladies.org`** (the Global
Leadership Team), or in Slack contact **`@Community Management Team`**. People asked to
stop harassing behaviour must comply immediately; leadership may take any action up to
**expulsion from all RLadies+ spaces** and identifying the person as a harasser. The
escalation detail, the allowed/not-allowed specifics, and how the CoC source is kept in
sync (GitHub source + Netlify build hooks; changes need global-leadership agreement) are
in [references/coc-and-policy.md](references/coc-and-policy.md).
