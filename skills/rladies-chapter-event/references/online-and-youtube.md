# Online events (shared Zoom) & YouTube uploads

Deep reference for `rladies-chapter-event`. Sources: `organizers/events/online`,
`online-no-zoom`, `youtube`.

## Shared RLadies+ Zoom

A Zoom Pro account (up to 100 participants) is available for chapter meetups.

**Booking**

- Book a slot via the **Calendly link in the `#online_meetups` channel** of the RLadies+
  Organizer Slack — **do not share this link publicly**. Availability is 24/7. You'll get
  an email with the Zoom link.
- On Meetup, fill in the event and paste **the exact link from the Calendly email**.
  **Do not connect your Zoom account to Meetup** — it would swap in a personal-account
  link.
- You need two codes, both in `#online_meetups`: the **passcode** (per-timeslot, lets
  people in) and the **host key** (lets you claim host). The host key is posted monthly.

**The 15-minute buffer**

"Join before host" is on and the waiting room is off (organizers are in many timezones).
To avoid trolls crashing before you arrive, **book 15 minutes early** (e.g. book 5:45pm,
tell people 6:00pm) and be punctual.

**Claim host with the host key**

1. Join the meeting with the passcode.
2. Open the **Participants** list → at the bottom click **Claim Host**.
3. Enter the **host key** (from `#online_meetups`; don't share it).

**After claiming host**

- Enable the **waiting room**; admit co-organizers first and make them **co-hosts** (they
  manage chat/bad actors, and can reclaim host if you drop). One co-host minimum; 3–4 for
  large events.
- Cross-reference the waiting room against the Meetup RSVP list (ask members to use real
  names + photos).
- Add "Organizer - " to your name. Make the presenter a co-host so they can screen-share.
- Enable **closed captions** (CC icon).
- Defaults already prevent most trolling (waiting room, muted on entry, removed
  participants can't rejoin, no file transfer/private messages/screen-share/virtual
  backgrounds for participants).

**Trolls**: remove the participant (hover → more → remove), **lock the meeting** (more →
lock), and **email `reporting@rladies.org`** after.

**Privacy & recording**: remind attendees of the CoC at the start. Ask permission before
saving the chat; ask people not to screenshot. To record/livestream, **ask the speaker
first**, note it in the event description, and announce before recording (camera-off =
opt out). Prefer **local recording**. For YouTube livestream, email
`youtube@rladies.org` **at least two weeks ahead**; you get temporary channel access (can
be unlisted).

**Socials**: use breakout rooms (3–4 people, 15–20 min, a board member per room if
possible); give a fun R-related prompt. Outside Zoom, `gather.town` is an option.

**End** the meeting (don't just leave) so the next organizer can use the room.

## Low-bandwidth alternative (no Zoom)

Email the presentation and set a time for **Q&A in a WhatsApp group** (RLadies+ Cotonou's
approach). Still remind everyone of the CoC and make organizers recognisable.

## YouTube uploads

Get a chapter recording onto the **RLadies+ Global** channel via the form at
<https://rladies.org/form/youtube>. After verification, the Google account you gave is
made a **channel manager** (switch to the RLadies+ account in YouTube Studio).

- **Title** has three parts: chapter name, language in parentheses, talk title — e.g.
  **`RLadies+ Freiburg (English) - R Markdown`** (check the hyphens).
- **Description** (in the content language is fine): describe the content (the event
  description works); **credit the speaker** — correct name spelling and pronouns, link
  their online presence if they want; link slides/materials and any resources mentioned
  in Q&A.
- In **More options**, set the recording date and language, and **add the video to the
  playlist for its language**.
- The same form is used to have the Global channel subscribe to a chapter channel or add
  a video to a playlist. Stuck? `youtube@rladies.org`.
