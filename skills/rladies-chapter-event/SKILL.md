# Running an RLadies+ Chapter Event

Plan ahead: **find speakers at least two months before the talk**, and sketch the
semester/year in advance even if it changes. Write event copy in the
[RLadies+ voice](../rladies-brand/SKILL.md); make graphics with `rladies-branded-assets`.

## Find and support a speaker

- **Recruit**: tell attendees you're looking; suggest topics to members ("you wrote a
  package — talk about it"); run lightning talks to lower the barrier; post a call on the
  Community Slack / socials / the Meetup group; tap your network; give a talk yourself
  sometimes.
- **Diversity is a rule, not a nicety**: avoid a year of all-white speakers and avoid
  all-white panels (3+ panelists). Reach beyond your locale, especially online, and into
  minority-focused communities. The verbatim rule and a directory of where to find
  diverse speakers are in [references/speakers.md](references/speakers.md).
- **Collect**: a bio, **their pronouns**, an informative abstract (content + format), and
  how many people they can present to. For online, **test screen-sharing ahead of time**.
- **Fund** travel/accommodation where you can, and make sure it's accessible.
- **After**: thank them, and encourage them (or offer) to submit their materials to
  R Weekly.

## Promote it

Consider **advertising to RLadies+ first** (Community/chapter Slack) so attendance isn't
all the majority gender — mind local equality law. Write a fun, informative title and a
Meetup summary with the speaker bio (validated by them); say whether it's hands-on,
whether kids/no-laptop folks are welcome. **Add alt text** to every graphic, make the
post text informative, link the Meetup event, and tag `@RLadiesGlobal`.

## Day-of checklist

- Arrive **15–30 min early**; check projector/mic/connections; have a holding slide
  (start time, wifi, setup steps).
- **Open with intro slides**: welcome, the RLadies+ mission, point to Slack/socials, and
  **remind everyone of the Code of Conduct** — refer to the written CoC and say it covers
  all media. Introduce the speaker **with their pronouns**.
- **Name tags with space for names _and_ pronouns**; make organizers recognisable
  (introduce yourselves / badge) so people can ask for help or report incidents.
- Snacks if a sponsor funds them (cater for dietary needs); keep the venue accessible.

A big turnout isn't the measure of success.

## Online events (shared RLadies+ Zoom)

There's a Zoom Pro account (100 participants). The full step-by-step — Calendly booking,
the **host key**, the 15-minute buffer trick, claiming host, co-hosts, captions, troll
handling, recording/livestream permissions, and "**end** the meeting, don't just
leave" — is in [references/online-and-youtube.md](references/online-and-youtube.md). Key
points: book via the Calendly link in `#online_meetups` (**never share it**); put that
exact link on Meetup (don't connect your personal Zoom); remind attendees of the CoC at
the start; report trolls to `reporting@rladies.org`. Low-bandwidth alternative: email the
slides and set a WhatsApp/email Q&A time.

## YouTube

Upload chapter recordings to the RLadies+ Global channel via
<https://rladies.org/form/youtube> (you're made a channel manager). Title format
**`RLadies+ City (Language) - Talk title`**; in the description, **credit the speaker
correctly (name spelling + pronouns)** and link their slides/materials; add the video to
the language playlist. Help: `youtube@rladies.org`. Details in
[references/online-and-youtube.md](references/online-and-youtube.md).

## Thank speakers and sponsors

Keep a list (name, support type, address, who sends the thank-you, sent/not — GDPR
aware). Send cards once a year (a sticker is a nice touch) or thank by email; remind
recipients what they're being thanked for.
