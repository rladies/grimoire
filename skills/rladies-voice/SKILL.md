# The RLadies+ Voice

The full voice guide: how RLadies+ talks, and how to keep an LLM-assisted
draft from reading like one wrote it. [`rladies-brand`](../rladies-brand/SKILL.md)
covers the rest of the identity — naming, the non-negotiable inclusive-language
rule, visual identity, and accessibility. Load this skill whenever you're
writing or reviewing prose for RLadies+ — a blog intro, a social caption, an
event announcement, a Slack reply, an invitation.

## The voice

> We sound like a friend who's excited about what the community is doing — not a
> corporation issuing announcements. Our voice is direct, warm, and grounded.
> We're a movement but we don't take ourselves too seriously.

**Do**

- Use "we" and "our" — we're part of the community, not above it.
- Lead with what's happening, not who we are.
- Keep it short — punchy lines, not paragraphs.
- Name people and chapters specifically — spotlight others, not the org.
- End with a clear call to action (link, sign-up, "come say hi").
- Use the purple heart 💜 as our signature emoji.

**Don't**

- Sound corporate, formal, or institutional.
- Use jargon or acronyms without context.
- Over-explain — trust the reader.
- Centre the global org over the people and chapters.
- Use exclamation marks excessively — one per post max.
- Say "females" or "women only" — the full inclusive-language rule ("gender
  minorities in R") is in `rladies-brand`.

## The tells to strip

- **Stock openers** — "In today's fast-paced world of data science...", "In the
  ever-evolving landscape of...", "Picture this:". Open with the concrete fact
  instead — a name, a date, a number.
- **Adjective triads** — "innovative, dynamic, and impactful"; "robust,
  scalable, and inclusive". Replace the pileup with one real, specific detail.
- **"Not just X, it's Y"** — "This isn't just a meetup, it's a movement." Say
  what it actually is.
- **Hedging** — "can potentially help", "may in some cases", "some might
  argue". State the thing directly; if you're genuinely unsure, say what you
  do know instead of padding around it.
- **Corporate-enthusiasm inflation** — "we are beyond thrilled and incredibly
  excited to announce...". One clear sentence beats three excited ones — this
  is the one-exclamation-mark-max rule above, showing up at the paragraph
  level.
- **Empty transitions** — "Moreover,", "Furthermore,", "It's worth noting
  that,", "In conclusion,". Cut them; the next sentence should stand on its
  own without a connective crutch.
- **Generic CTAs** — "Don't miss out!", "Stay tuned!". Swap in the specific
  action — a date, a link, a name to DM — per the "clear call to action"
  rule above.
- **Bullet-itis** — turning every paragraph into a bolded-lead-in bullet list
  ("**Community:** ...", "**Impact:** ..."). If it reads fine as a sentence,
  leave it as a sentence.
- **Inspirational-poster adjectives** — "vibrant, diverse, empowering
  community", "fostering an inclusive space". RLadies+ shows this by naming
  real people and chapters, never by adjective — see the "spotlight people
  and chapters, not the org" rule above.
- **Uniform rhythm** — every sentence the same medium length and shape. Read
  it aloud; vary it the way you'd actually talk.
- **Em dash / semicolon overload** — one or two per piece reads fine, wall to
  wall is a tell. Break into short lines instead — the "punchy lines, not
  paragraphs" rule above.
- **Summary endings** — "In summary, X is a great way to Y." End on the call
  to action, not a recap of what you just said.

## Before / after

**Blog intro**

> ❌ In today's rapidly evolving data science landscape, the R programming
> language continues to be an indispensable tool for practitioners across the
> globe. RLadies+ is thrilled to announce an exciting new initiative that will
> empower our vibrant community of gender minorities in the R ecosystem.
>
> ✅ RLadies Oslo ran its first hybrid meetup last month — 40 people showed up,
> half of them on Zoom across three time zones. Here's what worked, and what
> we're changing next time.

**Social post**

> ❌ We are incredibly excited to announce our upcoming event! Whether you're a
> beginner or an experienced R user, this session has something for everyone.
> Don't miss out on this amazing opportunity to learn and connect! 🎉📊✨
>
> ✅ Marie's teaching {sf} basics at RLadies Nairobi on June 18 — bring a
> laptop, no maps experience needed. Link in bio. 💜 #RLadies #RStats

**Event announcement**

> ❌ We are proud to present a truly remarkable lineup of diverse speakers for
> our upcoming conference, showcasing the incredible talent within our
> community.
>
> ✅ Three speakers, three continents, one Zoom link: Amara, Priya, and Sol on
> June 18. Sign up below.

**Community Slack reply (moderation)**

> ❌ Thank you for reaching out. We understand your concerns and want to
> assure you that we take this matter very seriously. Our team is committed to
> fostering a safe and inclusive environment for all members.
>
> ✅ Thanks for flagging this — you're right, that crossed a line. I've removed
> the message and I'm following up with them directly.

## Before you ship it

- Read it aloud. If you wouldn't say it in the community Slack, cut it.
- Count the exclamation marks — one, max.
- Find the call to action. Is it a name, date, or link — or does it just say
  "don't miss out"?
- Could you delete a sentence without losing information? Delete it.
- Would a chapter organizer actually send this to a friend?
