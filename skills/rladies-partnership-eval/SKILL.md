# Evaluating an RLadies+ Partnership

Decide through one lens — **does this advance gender diversity in the R community and
build collaborative, independent community, or is it extractive / commercial?** Run the
request through the rules gate first (any failure is disqualifying), then the
diversity-washing judgement.

## The rules gate (a failure here is a no)

- **Free** — public RLadies+ events are always free to participants; a partner's
  product/service offered at the event must be free to all attending, with no fees.
- **R-specific** — the scope is the open-source R community.
- **No commercial agenda** — chapters/events may not be _used_ as a commercial vehicle.
  Partners must not openly market or sell during the event, and **speakers can't talk
  about paid products/services** (even if they don't work for the vendor).
- **Independence** — RLadies+ keeps no permanent ties to sponsors; support is welcome
  only if it doesn't seek to dictate or influence mission-driven decisions. Sponsorship
  is fine as venue/food/supplies; chapters are started by individuals, not companies.
- **Leadership & speakers** — organiser/mentor roles are held by minority-gender people;
  minority-gender speakers are preferred where there's sufficient choice.
- **Code of Conduct** — applies to everyone, everywhere.

## The diversity-washing judgement

> Assess whether the organization is looking for "diversity points" — getting good
> publicity for being connected to RLadies+, but not invested in true collaboration.

- Ask others who've worked with them **and have no conflict of interest** (not their
  staff).
- Check whether they include and support **groups systematically excluded from tech**.

**For a conference / co-hosted event specifically, check:**

- Does the event have a **code of conduct**? If not, will they add one?
- Are people from systematically-excluded groups part of it, and in what role
  (keynote, organiser, panelist, chair)? If not, will they change that? (Avoid all-white
  panels and line-ups — hold partners to what we ask of others.)
- Agree which **Code of Conduct** applies, and each side's **gender policy** for
  organisers/speakers, in advance.

## Precedent: DataCamp

There can be **no partnership between RLadies+ (including chapters) and DataCamp**, and
we don't promote or offer its products — even free ones — on official channels. It's the
standing example of declining a partner on ethical grounds (see the 2019 blog
statements). Use it as the reference point when a partner's conduct is the issue.

## Approving RLadies+ branded conference participation

An "RLadies+ branded event" uses "RLadies+" in the title; a directory member may lead
one **with global-team approval**. They email `info@rladies.org` with the approval
template; invited speakers must have **all fees waived/reimbursed and travel, lodging, or
meals covered**. Past events are tracked in
[rladies_global_presentations](https://github.com/rladies/rladies_global_presentations).
The exact rules text, the 10 core values, and the conference email templates are in
[references/rules-values-templates.md](references/rules-values-templates.md).
